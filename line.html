Line

By Gianne Alcantara

Love that crosses the line,
a tangled, thorny vine,
Where passion burns so bright
And choke the very breath
Leaving only ashes, in a desolate space.

To learn to let go, a painful, slow release,
Of clinging, desperate hands, that crave a fragile peace.
To sever every thread, that binds you to the past,
To break the chains of hurt, that hold you ever fast.

To forgive the bitter words, the actions sharp and keen,
To understand the flaws, the darkness in between.
To see the human heart, with all its frailty shown,
And find a space for mercy, on a path you walk alone.

To move on, not forget, but rise above the pain,
To heal the wounded soul, and find yourself again.
To build a stronger self, from fragments scattered wide,
And open up your heart, to love that will abide.

Love that crosses the line, can leave a lasting scar,
But from the ashes rise, a love that shines afar.
A love that's true and deep, a love that understands,
The power of forgiveness, held within your hands.
