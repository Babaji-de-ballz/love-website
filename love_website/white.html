White

Too much light can blind the eye,
A love too pure can terrify.
For in its glow, we lose control,
And love consumes the trembling soul.

Like moths to flame, we're drawn to its might,
Helpless to resist, its radiant light.
It burns away, our fears, our doubts, our pain,
And leaves us raw, with love's sweet, tender stain.

In love's pure fire, we're refined and tried,
Our hearts transformed, our souls inside.
It's a crucible, that tests our will,
And forges us, into a love that's strong and still.

Yet even as it terrifies, it sets us free,
A liberation, that's both wild and carefree.
For in its light, we find our truest selves,
And a love that's pure, that forever dwells.
